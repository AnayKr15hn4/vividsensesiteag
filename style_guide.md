# VividSense Brand Style Guide

This style guide outlines the core design elements of the VividSense brand. Use this as a reference when creating social media visuals, videos, presentations, or any other marketing materials to ensure a consistent and premium brand identity.

## 1. Typography

Consistent typography is crucial for maintaining the VividSense identity.

- **Headings & Titles:** **Outfit**
  - _Usage:_ Use for large text in videos, thumbnails, and primary headings. It has a modern, clean, and geometric feel.
- **Body & Subtitles:** **Inter**
  - _Usage:_ Use for longer paragraphs, captions, lower-thirds, and secondary information. Highly legible and neutral.

## 2. Color Palette

The VividSense aesthetic is predominantly high-contrast, clean, and modern, with specific accent colors to draw attention.

### Core Colors

- **Primary Background:** White (`#ffffff`) or Off-White
- **Primary Text/Foreground:** True Black (`#000000`) or Dark Gray (`#050505` / `#0a0a0a`)
- **Neutral/Surfaces:** Light Gray (`#f5f5f5`, `#f9f9f9`)

### Accent Colors

- **Brand Teal:** Cyber/Electric Teal (`#00ced1`)
- **Glow Blue/Action:** Deep Blue/Cyan glow for emphasis (e.g., `rgba(0, 102, 255, 0.5)`)

## 3. Visual Textures & Effects

To match the website's premium and slightly cinematic aesthetic, apply these effects sparingly but intentionally in your visuals:

- **Grain & Noise:** The brand uses a subtle film grain/noise texture overlay to give depth and a tactile feel. In videos or static posts, overlay a very low-opacity noise effect (around 10-15%).
- **Glassmorphism (Frosted Glass):** Use semi-transparent backgrounds with a heavy background blur when creating cards, floating elements, or text overlays.
  - _Characteristics:_ Slight white tint (`5-10%` opacity), strong blur, and a very thin, subtle white border (`10%` opacity).
- **Text Glows:** For highly emphasized text or tech-focused neon aesthetics, use a soft, wide drop-shadow using an electric blue or teal color to create a glowing effect.

## 4. Motion & Animation (For Videos)

When creating motion graphics or video edits, mimic the website's smooth and precise animations:

- **Fade-Ins & Slide-Ups:** Elements shouldn't snap into place. They should smoothly fade in and float up slightly (e.g., translating upwards by `20px` while fading from `0%` to `100%` over `0.5s`).
- **Easing:** Use graceful "ease-out" curves. The movement should start fast and decelerate smoothly into its final resting position.
- **Smooth Pacing:** Avoid frantic cuts. The pacing should feel deliberate, premium, and sophisticated.

## 5. Overall Vibe & Keywords

- Premium, Clean, Technical, Cinematic, High-Contrast, Minimalist, Forward-Thinking.
